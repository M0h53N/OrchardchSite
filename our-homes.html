Our Homes

Providing safe, nurturing environments across the East Midlands

Apple Tree

Established 2017 Ofsted Outstanding
Capacity: Up to 4 young people
Age Range: 6-19 years
Gender: Mixed

Established in 2017 and rated Outstanding by Ofsted, this therapeutic EBD (Emotional and Behavioural Difficulties) home provides high-quality care and support for up to four young people. As a mixed-gender provision, the home accommodates children and young people aged 6 to 19 with complex and additional needs, including attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), autism spectrum disorder (ASD), Global Developmental Delay, and various processing difficulties.

The care team is committed to creating a nurturing, safe, and structured environment where each young person can thrive. Predictability, routine, and consistency are at the heart of the home's approach, alongside a wide range of engaging activities designed to build social and emotional skills. Through therapeutic support and a personalised care model, the home empowers young people to develop resilience, confidence, and positive relationships.

Birch Tree

Established May 2021 Ofsted Outstanding
Capacity: Up to 3 young people
Age Range: Wide range supported
Gender: Mixed

Open since May 2021 and rated Outstanding by Ofsted, this therapeutic EBD (Emotional and Behavioural Difficulties) home provides care for up to three young people in a mixed-gender setting. The home is designed to support young people across a wide age range, allowing each individual to focus on their personal milestones and developmental needs without pressure or comparison.

The care team has specialist expertise in supporting young people who are at risk of sexual and criminal exploitation, as well as those from younger age groups who have experienced severe neglect. With a trauma-informed and child-centred approach, the team offers a structured, nurturing, and therapeutic environment where safety, trust, and emotional growth are prioritised.

Every aspect of care is tailored to support recovery, build resilience, and empower young people to move forward with confidence and stability.

Cherry Tree

Established 2022 Ofsted Registered
Capacity: 2 young people
Age Range: 7-17 years
Gender: Mixed

Cherry Tree has been operating since 2022 and is registered with Ofsted. It is a therapeutic two-bed residential home that provides specialist care for children and young people aged 7 to 17 years. The home caters to individuals of mixed genders and is designed to offer a safe, nurturing, and structured environment for those who have experienced trauma-based abuse.

The dedicated care team at Cherry Tree is trained in trauma-informed practice and specialises in supporting young people with complex emotional and behavioural needs. Through consistent, therapeutic one-to-one support, staff work to create a sense of safety, trust, and emotional stability for each young person. The aim is to foster resilience, promote healthy attachments, and support the individual's journey toward recovery and positive long-term outcomes.

Dewberry

Ofsted Registered
Capacity: 4 young people
Age Range: 6-18 years
Gender: Mixed

Dewberry House is a five-bedroom house which is registered with Ofsted to provide residential care for four young people with behavioural and emotional difficulties aged between 6 and 18 years, as identified in their personal Care Plans.

Although young people will be admitted due to behavioural difficulties, they may also have learning difficulties such as Asperger's, ADHD or ASD or other underlying issues such as self-injurious behaviours, trauma, and emotional disorders such as attachment issues. The staff team have completed training in these areas to ensure they have a good understanding and knowledge of this and can care for the young people and meet their needs.
